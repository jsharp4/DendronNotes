Storage Capacity

As described by Alan Turing in Computing Machinery and Intelligence, the storage capacity of a system is defined as

CS=log2(nds)C_S = log_2(n_{ds})CS​=log2​(nds​)

where ndsn_{ds}nds​ is the number of distinct discrete states in storage of the machine.

For example, a machine with one storage block of 1,024 bits has 210242^{1024}21024 states. Its storage capacity is

CS=log2(21024)=1024C_S = log_2(2^{1024}) = 1024CS​=log2​(21024)=1024, or one Kb.

Storage capacity is additive:

10 of the same 1 Kb storage nodes above yield a total storage capacity of 10 Kb.
